The Medium is the Message?

So what does Marshall McLuhan mean when saying "The Medium Is the Message"? It's not that the content of the message is irrelevant, but rather that the way in which we send & receive messages are much more important. With the advent of technologies like the internet and its various social platforms, have changed the way we behave. An immediate example to this would be how the change of the primary form of communication from oral conversations to printed words made us utilize our visual sense more than our hearing sense.

In modern day, with the internet as our medium, we have created new styles of communicating with one another. We now utilize internet slang such as "lol" and emojis to share our thoughts with one another. It has also given us a facade to hide behind because our real emotions and thoughts are hidden behind these lines of text, creating a false sense of security for the other party.

In a sense, Donald Trump has successfully imprinted this idea to his supporters through his labelling of 'fake news' during his reign as President of the United States. The distrust between the media (the medium) and the citizens (the consumers) are at an all time high. The actual news (the content) is no longer the focus as people will always hold the idea of "fake news" in their heads in some type of form whenever they tune in to the news.

Social Media
Personal Examples

A personal example regarding behavior on social media, I feel obligated to reply with a "lol" to something that was sent to me even if I do not find it humourous or if I'm in a bad mood. Vice versa, receiving a simple 'lol' reply would make me feel more comfortable about not having wasted the other person's time. Of course this doesn't mean that prior to the invention of internet people didn't put on a front in the presence of one another, but the tendency and ease of doing so definitely changed.

McLuhan mentions how the future medium will be "One big gossip column that is unforgiving, unforgetful and from which there is no redemption". This reminded me of the Black Lives Matter movement that occured a couple months back. During the height of the movement, some former student at my former high school decided to dig up all posts and comments of other former students that had any hints of racial discrimination. From people calling each other "monkey" to others using the "n-word", no one was spared unless they formally and publically "apologize". To put it simply "cancel culture" happened and the witch hunt did nothing to help the movement and instead only made the real racists hide in the shadow while making people afraid of what is allowed to be said online.

Fun Facts
  • McLuhan proposed this idea in 1967, decades before the web was created/massively used.
  • The book's title The Medium is The Massage is a printing error that McLuhan kept because he thought it was fitting.
  • McLuhan claimed to have only read the right hand page of all books because he found the contents of books to be redundant.
  • “When I first came on the scene, in 1990, no one talked about McLuhan — it was as if he had never existed, and when I spoke about him there was no traction,” Camille Paglia, professor of English at the University of the Arts in Philadelphia, told the New York Times in 2000.
